Ewa Agonyin

Ewa Agonyin is a popular Nigerian street food made from mashed beans served with a spicy, smoky pepper sauce. Loved for its rich flavor and soft texture, it's perfect with bread, yam, or plantain.

Recipe Information

Preparation time: 10 minutes

Cooking time: 1 hour 10 minutes

Servings: 4

Difficulty: Beginner

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of black-eyed beans
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tsp salt (to taste)
  • 6–8 medium dried red chilies (tatashe or shombo)
  • 4 fresh Scotch bonnet peppers (ata rodo)
  • 2 medium onions
  • 1 cup palm oil
  • 2 seasoning cubes

Instructions

  1. Rinse beans thoroughly and place in a large pot.
  2. Add water to cover and boil until very soft (45–60 minutes).
  3. Mash cooked beans slightly and set aside.
  4. Soak dried chilies in hot water for 10–15 minutes.
  5. Blend soaked chilies, Scotch bonnet peppers, bell pepper, and 1 onion until smooth.
  6. Heat palm oil until clear, then fry sliced onion until caramelized.
  7. Add blended pepper mix and fry on low heat for 20–25 minutes until dark and oil separates.
  8. Season with salt and seasoning cubes.
  9. Serve mashed beans with pepper sauce on top.

Tips

For authentic flavor, fry the sauce until it turns dark and use plenty of palm oil. The beans should be very soft for the signature texture.
